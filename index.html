The Gold Standard

What is the ADOS-2 Assessment?

The Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule, Second Edition (ADOS-2) is widely considered the most accurate tool for diagnosing Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). It is not a simple "test" with right or wrong answers, but a semi-structured observation of communication, social interaction, and play.

Why we use ADOS-2

  • Objectivity: Removes bias by using standardized toys and activities.

  • Comprehensive: Evaluates communication, social interaction, play, and restricted behaviors.

  • Insurance Preferred: Most insurance carriers require ADOS-2 scores for authorizing ABA therapy.

How it compares to other tests

Assessment Type Accuracy
ADOS-2 Clinical Observation Highest (Gold Standard)
CARS-2 Rating Scale High
M-CHAT Parent Questionnaire Screening Only

*Note: While the M-CHAT is a great screening tool for pediatricians, it cannot provide a formal medical diagnosis. An ADOS-2 evaluation is required for a complete diagnosis.

Signs & Symptoms by Age

Autism presents differently as a child grows. Use this guide to identify potential red flags.

Toddlers

12-24 Months
  • No babbling or pointing by age 1
  • Poor eye contact or smiling
  • Does not respond to name
  • Lines up toys instead of playing

Preschool

2-5 Years
  • Delayed speech / Echolalia (repeating)
  • Difficulty playing "pretend"
  • Extreme tantrums over minor changes
  • Hand flapping or toe walking

School Age

6+ Years
  • Struggles to make or keep friends
  • Intense interest in specific topics
  • Misses social cues or sarcasm
  • Sensory sensitivities (loud noises)
Discuss your child's symptoms with a specialist
